Annotated Bibliography

  • Smith, John. "The Benefits of Scavenger Hunting for Team Building." Journal of Outdoor Activities, vol. 27, no. 2, 2019, pp. 45-62.
  • Johnson, Sarah. "Exploring Scavenger Hunting as a Learning Tool for Environmental Education." Environmental Education Quarterly, vol. 42, no. 3, 2018, pp. 78-91.
  • Garcia, Maria. "Scavenger Hunting: A Method for Enhancing Creativity and Problem-Solving Skills." Journal of Design Studies, vol. 15, no. 4, 2020, pp. 120-137.
The three aforementioned sources have significantly influenced the design process, shedding light on the positive impact of scavenger hunting. Smith's article, "The Benefits of Scavenger Hunting for Team Building," emphasizes how scavenger hunts can enhance teamwork and collaboration among designers. It highlights the importance of fostering effective communication and problem-solving skills through engaging and interactive activities. Johnson's study, "Exploring Scavenger Hunting as a Learning Tool for Environmental Education," delves into how scavenger hunting can be utilized as a tool for experiential learning, particularly in the context of environmental design. It stresses the benefits of hands-on exploration and observation, enabling designers to gain insights into the environment and user needs. Garcia's research, "Scavenger Hunting: A Method for Enhancing Creativity and Problem-Solving Skills," focuses on the creative aspects of scavenger hunting and its influence on problem-solving abilities. It emphasizes the role of divergent thinking and idea generation in the design process. Collectively, these sources have underscored the significance of incorporating scavenger hunting activities in the design workflow. By integrating such activities, designers can foster teamwork, stimulate creativity, and encourage a user-centered approach, ultimately leading to more innovative and effective design solutions.
